paper mache dog

in elementary school my cousin had a birthday party at some art studio and we made paper mache things. I made a brown dog with floppy ears and a magenta butt. My mom wasn't a big fan of that detail. Eventually, she had me get rid of it. But I never forgot it. This is my tribute to him.
I made the mache mix with a 1:1 flour/water mix, one cup each, and then I added roughly 1/4 cup of mod podge.
Also, I want to make a 3d model of this guy. I'm going to download an app to try to scan it in 3d.

2025-01-12
pocket shrine

A craft escapade generated by boredom. I polled my friends on instagram and asked who should be the subject of the shrine. I landed on willem dafoe. I found an image online I liked and added a threshold to it in PS, which I stenciled on lino to carve a print. I did some beading and embroidery, all of which I freestyled without any real knowledge of proper technique, and I think it turned out alright. I hand sewed the base cross shape using scrap fabric I found. The two crosses I made with air dry clay, and on one I created a ring with wire at the top and threaded through rosary-esque beads which I sewed into the inside of the pocket to anchor it. I also used a canadian coin (in efforts to replicate the metal charms sometimes found on these pocket shrines), and attached a wire loop to the top and thread it into the left arm.

2025-01-11
Gelli plate first experiments

Just got my first gelli plate. it took a lot of trial and error to get a clear image. I found that my smo were too thin and dried too fast, but my rollerball ink had some success. I might buy a heavy body acrylic and see how that works. So far, the best process I have found is ~15 seconds of smoothing the magazine page onto the plate with light pressure, and then letting the plate sit on the final paper for at least a couple minutes. Pulling it off too soon means it will not dry and will not stick to the paper.
